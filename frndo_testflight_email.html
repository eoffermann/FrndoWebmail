Better Input, Better Conversations — New Build Ready

March 2026  ·  Release Notes for TestFlight Testers

Hello, and thank you for continuing to test Frndo. This latest build is focused on making the conversation experience feel more responsive and polished — how you talk to Frndo, how you type to Frndo, and how Frndo shows you what it heard. Here's what's changed.

✦  New to Frndo? Here's the short version

Frndo is an AI companion app — a place to talk, reflect, and connect with a thoughtful, expressive avatar that's designed to feel genuinely present. Whether you want someone to talk through your day with, practice a difficult conversation, or simply have a friendly presence on-screen, Frndo is built for that.

It's for anyone who values meaningful interaction — people who are curious about where AI companionship is heading, or who simply appreciate having a patient, non-judgmental conversational partner available whenever they want one.

🛠 What's New in This Build
🎙

Push-to-Talk Improvements

The Push-to-Talk button now properly stops listening when you turn it off — previously it could keep the mic active even after being disabled. You'll also notice a clear on/off status indicator, a satisfying press animation, and haptic vibration feedback when you tap the button, so you always know the mic state at a glance.
💬

Live Speech Transcription

When you speak to Frndo, your words now appear on screen as live text — so you can see exactly what the app heard you say. It's a small addition that makes the conversation feel much more grounded and transparent.
⌨️

Revamped Text Input

Typing longer messages is now a first-class experience. The input field grows dynamically as you write, a scrollbar appears for especially long messages, and there's a proper send button for when you're ready. We also fixed an annoying bug where deleting text with backspace could leave a phantom blank line behind.
🔗

New Suggestion UI

The old media box has been replaced with a cleaner, purpose-built interface for sharing suggested links and URLs during a conversation. It's simpler, more intuitive, and gets out of the way when you don't need it.
🧠

Smarter Memory on the Backend

We've made improvements to how Frndo consolidates and manages conversational memory behind the scenes. You won't see this one directly, but over time it means Frndo will do a better job of remembering context across your conversations.
